Gender in Local Politics

A gender breakdown of London Borough Councils

Gender issues have dominated the news agenda in recent months - whether the story has been about sexual assault, the gender pay gap, or women in business. But what about local politics?

New research has revealed that in the capital, 39% of councillors are women.

The data, from an analysis of local council websites, shows that there are 762 female councillors, compared to 1197 men, in London.

Yet although this demonstrates that men dominate the capital’s politics, the figure is above the national average.

A Fawcett Society report in 2017 found that just 33% of councillors in the UK were women. This represents just a 5% increase in female representation since 1997, leading the report to conclude that “local government is still, overwhelmingly, a man-made male-dominated world.”

Similarly, the UK Parliament saw a record number of female MPs elected in 2017 - but this figure remains just 32% of MPs.

The London figures do, however, show a wide disparity in female representation across the capital’s councils.

Lambeth Council has the most female councillors, with 48% of the council women. In contrast, Havering Council has the fewest - only a quarter are female.





Frances Scott from 50:50 Parliament, an organisation campaigning for gender balance at Westminster, said that that the figures were positive but "more needs to be done".

"It is good to see that women are participating in local councils. 50:50 would like better representation of women at a local level and think that it is important that all our democratic processes and political institutions are equally accessible and inclusive of women.

"But the statistics still reveal the historic, institutional sexism and it is clear that more needs to be done."

"Local politics is one route to Westminster so it is great to see women participating, and being able to participate, at a local level. But the statistics still reveal the historic, institutional sexism and it is clear that more needs to be done."


There is also a large variation in the number of female councillors according to political party. Labour has the most women, with 42% of their councillors female. Of Liberal Democrat councillors, 39% are women, while 33% of Conservatives are female.
